Income · Expenses · Profit

The three numbers every business owner must know. ↓

💰 Income Money coming in to your business — from sales, services, or any revenue source.
📤 Expenses Money leaving your business — rent, supplies, salaries, software, marketing, etc.
📈 Profit Income − Expenses. This is what you actually keep. If it's negative, you're losing money.

Startup Expenses

One-time costs to get your business off the ground. ↓

Startup Expenses

  • Equipment
  • LLC Filing Fees
  • Software
  • Vehicles
  • Utilities / Rent Deposit
  • Furniture

These are paid once to open your doors — not every month.

Revenue Assumptions

Before you can project profit, estimate your sales:

  • Estimated sales per month
  • Per product or service
  • Average price per sale for each product or service

Revenue Assumption = Units Sold × Average Price

Cost of Service

What does it actually cost you to deliver the service?

Materials & Supplies — everything you consume or use up per job.

Example: a painter pays for paint, tape, and brushes on every project.

This is separate from your fixed monthly costs.

Operating Expenses

The recurring costs of keeping your business running. ↓

Monthly Operating Expenses

  • Monthly Rent
  • Office Supplies
  • Salaries
  • Marketing
  • Utilities
  • Insurance

These recur every month whether or not you make a sale.

Breakeven Point

The breakeven point is the level of sales at which your expenses equal your revenue — you're not losing money, but you're not profiting yet either.

Below breakeven → losing money.

Above breakeven → profit.

What Is an LLC?

A Limited Liability Company (LLC) is a business structure that protects your personal assets — like your home or savings — from business debts and lawsuits.

If someone sues your business, they can't come after your personal bank account.

It's one of the first things most entrepreneurs set up.

Build Your Business Plan

8 sections. 30 minutes. Let's go. ↓

1. Description

In one sentence — what does your business do, and for whom?

2. Vision

Where is this going? What change do you want to create?

3. Target Audience

Who exactly are you serving?

Demographics + their pain points.

4. Competitive Analysis

Who are your competitors, and what are they doing?

5. Your Edge

What makes you different or better than them?

6. Marketing Plan

Flyers · SEO · Social media · Email · Phone calls · Local network

7. Pricing Strategy

Are you a budget provider or a premium one?

Both can win — pick one on purpose.

8. Financial Projections

  • Startup cost
  • Operating Expenses
  • Cost of Service
  • Income Projections

Create Your Website

Turn your business plan into a real website. ↓

How to Start

  1. Copy and paste your business plan into AI
  2. Describe the tone of your website
  3. Choose your approach:
  • Describe each component individually
  • Draw a wireframe on paper, then give it to AI
  • Let AI decide the layout

Iterate until you have something you love.

Website Components

Navbar Top of the page — links to sections
Header Describes your business + Call to Action
Services What you offer and how it helps
Qualifications Why should they trust you?

Pricing (optional) Your rates or packages

Contact Form How customers reach you
